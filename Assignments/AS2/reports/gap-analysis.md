# Resume Gap Analysis Report

## ✅ Your Strengths
*   Proficient in multiple programming languages (Java, JavaScript, Python, C++) which aligns with general software engineering fundamentals.
*   Strong experience with full-stack development, demonstrated by projects using React, Node.js, Express.js and related backend technologies.
*   Solid cloud deployment experience using AWS (EC2, ECS, ECR, Cognito), Docker, and CI/CD pipelines using GitHub Actions.
*   Experience with both relational (SQL, PostgreSQL, Oracle SQL, SQLite) and NoSQL databases (MongoDB, DynamoDB).
*   Familiarity with RESTful API development for Microservices.

## 🎯 Skill Gaps by Effort

### ⚡ Quick Wins (Do this week)

*   Software Engineering Fundamentals: Explicitly highlight your knowledge of data structures, algorithms, and object-oriented programming principles on your resume.

### 📅 Short-Term (Days to 2 weeks)

*   TypeScript: Complete the free TypeScript handbook at typescriptlang.org (~8 hrs) and convert one existing JS project to TS.

### 🔧 Medium-Term (Weeks to months)

*   Mobile Development (Swift/Kotlin): Complete a course on Swift or Kotlin (e.g., Codecademy, Udemy), build a basic mobile app, and add it to your GitHub portfolio.

### 🏗️ Long-Term (Months to years)

*   Bachelor's Degree in Computer Science: Seneca's degree completion pathway or Athabasca University's online CS degree are options — or prioritize roles that accept diplomas + experience.
*   Work Experience (1-5 years): Prioritize applying for junior-level roles and highlight internship/project experience to demonstrate practical skills relevant to the job requirements.

## 💎 What Makes You Stand Out

Your experience with JavaFX, a less common UI framework, may be appealing to older projects/companies. Your specific cloud deployment experience with Dockerized applications and secure Authentication (Basic Auth & Cognito JWT) also gives you an edge. These are great conversation-starters that show you can handle diverse tech stacks and real-world security.

## 📋 Top 5 Priorities

1.  **Software Engineering Fundamentals (Quick Win):** Articulating your foundational knowledge will subtly boost your perceived skill.
2.  **Work Experience (Long-Term):** Focus on junior roles and aggressively tailor your resume.
3.  **TypeScript (Short-Term):** TS is in high demand and is relatively easy to pick up; it will immediately make you more marketable.
4.  **Mobile Development (Medium-Term):** Mobile skills open many doors; a small investment here can pay off big.
5.  **Bachelor's Degree (Long-Term):** While important, weigh the time commitment against the immediate need to land a satisfying job.
